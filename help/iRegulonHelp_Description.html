iRegulon Help


User instructions

iRegulon is a Cytoscape plugin for visualizing a predicted gene regulatory network (GRN). The target of iRegulon is to visualize the output of the different flavors of CisTarget in Cytoscape.
CisTargetX and i-cisTarget predicts transcription factors (TFs), enhancers and target genes (TGs). The computational prediction of transcription factor binding sites (TFBSs) gives a large amount of false-positive predictions. To improve these predictions cisTarget uses the advantage of the clustering behavior of TFBSs in cis-regulatory modules (CRMs), the evolutionary conservation of binding sites and the presence of particular chromatin marks in CRMs.
Two types of approaches are used for the experimental validations of cisTarget. The first approach constructs well-defined enhancer models by using high-quality training sets of similar CRMs. The second approach combines gene expression data with genome-wide scoring to perform motif discovery and CRM prediction. These two methods combine motif clustering in CRMs, comparative genomics, genome-wide CRM scoring, gene co-expression, and use large collections of candidate position weight matrices (PWMs). The performance of correct identification of TF/TG interactions is high enough to allow resource-effective experimental validation.
For further information about how cisTarget works:
Potier, D., Atak, Z.K., Sanchez, M.N., Herrmann, C. and Aerts S. (2012). Methods in Molecular Biology vol. 786, Chapter 18: Using cisTargetX to Predict Transcriptional Targets and Networks in Drosophila. France: Springer Science
